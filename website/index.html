Features

Dictation that gets out of your way.

Built around a single muscle-memory move: press, speak, release. Everything else is automatic.

Hold-to-talk, anywhere

A global hotkey works system-wide — even in apps that don't support dictation natively. Tap-to-toggle is one keypress away.

AI cleanup, on the fly

Filler words removed, punctuation added, tone preserved. Powered by your choice of OpenAI-compatible model, with Groq fallback.

Screen-aware context

Knows your active window, selected text, and (optionally) a screenshot — so "fix this paragraph" actually fixes that paragraph.

Your keys, your audio

API keys live in macOS Keychain. Audio goes straight to the endpoint you configure — no Stro Speak servers in the loop.

Pipeline history

Every transcript is logged locally with timing and model details, so you can audit, replay, or copy a past dictation.

Configurable everything

Custom hotkeys, endpoint URLs, models, prompts. The defaults work — the knobs are there when you need them.

How it works

From key-down to keystrokes in one breath.

Four stages, all happening between the moment you start talking and the moment you stop.

  1. 01

    You press

    A global hotkey (default: hold fn) opens a mic stream and shows the recording overlay.

    fn
  2. 02

    You speak

    Audio is captured locally with level metering. Stop talking, release the key — recording ends instantly.

  3. 03

    AI cleans

    Transcribed by your chosen endpoint, then polished by an LLM that sees the active window for context.

  4. 04

    It pastes

    The cleaned text is dropped into the focused field — Slack, an email, your editor, anywhere.

    |
Shortcuts

Muscle memory in two keys.

Defaults are quiet and out of the way. Rebind anything in Settings if your fingers disagree.

  • fn
    Hold to talk Press and hold while you speak. Release to send.
  • ⌘+fn
    Tap to toggle Hands-free recording. Tap again to stop and paste.
  • esc
    Cancel Discard a recording without transcribing or pasting.

FAQ

Quick answers.

What macOS versions are supported?+

macOS 13 Ventura and later, on both Apple Silicon and Intel Macs.

Where does my audio go?+

Straight from your Mac to whichever OpenAI-compatible endpoint you've configured — by default, OpenAI. Nothing routes through Stro Speak's infrastructure.

Do I need an API key?+

Yes. You bring your own — from OpenAI, Groq, or any compatible provider. It's stored in macOS Keychain and never leaves your machine.

What permissions does it need?+

Microphone (to record), Accessibility (to paste into other apps), and optionally Screen Recording (to capture context). You'll be prompted on first launch.

Is it open source?+

The app is a refactor of FreeFlow (MIT). Check the repo for the current license and source.
